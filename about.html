About Ho Teaching Hospital

"Save a Life, Save the World."

Ho Teaching Hospital (HTH)

Ho Teaching Hospital (HTH) is the primary referral hospital in the Volta Region of Ghana. Our mission is to provide advanced clinical health services, support the training of undergraduate and postgraduate medical and other healthcare professionals, and undertake research into health issues for the purpose of improving health outcomes. As a strategic partner, we are committed to excellence in patient care and medical education. HTH has eight (8) Sub-Budget Management Centers (Sub-BMCs) and sixteen (16) Directorates.

Our Motto

Save a Life, Save the World.

Referral Center

The leading tertiary care facility for the Volta Region and beyond.

Brief History of Ho Teaching Hospital

1998

Construction Begins

Construction of the facility, then known as the Volta Regional Hospital, commenced with funding from the British Government.

2000

Commissioning

The hospital was commissioned in December 2000 and began its operations, quickly establishing itself as a key healthcare provider.

2019

Teaching Hospital Status

Designated as a Teaching Hospital in April 2019 by the Ministry of Health, enhancing its role in medical education and research.

Present

Continuous Expansion

Ongoing expansion of services and infrastructure to meet the growing demands for specialized healthcare in the region.
